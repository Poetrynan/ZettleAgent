# Emergence

Emergence occurs when a system suddenly exhibits capabilities it did not previously possess after crossing a certain scale threshold.

## Emergence in LLMs

When model parameters exceed critical thresholds, new capabilities spontaneously appear:

* **Chain-of-Thought reasoning**

* **Code generation**

* **Multilingual translation** (never explicitly trained)

* **Arithmetic reasoning**

These abilities were never explicitly programmed — they **emerged** from large-scale training.

## Analogy with Zettelkasten

Knowledge emergence in the [[Zettelkasten Method]]:

* A few dozen notes → just notes

* A few hundred notes → unexpected connections begin to appear

* A few thousand notes → **new ideas emerge spontaneously**

This mirrors the [[Scaling Laws]] observed in the [[GPT Series]]: quantitative change triggers qualitative transformation.

## Complex Systems Perspective

Emergence is a universal property of complex systems:

* Ant colony intelligence — no single ant is intelligent

* Neural networks — no single neuron can think

* Knowledge networks — no single note generates insight
